LOGISTICS PROJECT

This logistics dashboard shows strong overall performance: $262.53M revenue across 107 customers, 120 trucks, and 85.41K trips. Contract freight ($98.8M) leads revenue by customer type, with Automotive the top freight category. However, the average on-time delivery rate of just 0.45 is a major red flag, signalling operational inefficiency despite solid revenue. Driver metrics show experienced staff (12.46 yrs avg) but wide variance in fuel usage and mileage revenue. Maintenance costs ($1.82M) and 24.96-hour average downtime, concentrated in a few trucks (TRK00073, TRK00099), suggest targeted fleet maintenance could reduce costs and improve delivery reliability significantly. Monthly trends reveal seasonal dips (February, September) in both load revenue and driver trips, warranting further investigation into causes—possibly weather, holidays, or reduced demand. Top drivers like DRV00142 and DRV00013 generate disproportionately high mileage revenue, indicating an opportunity to study and replicate their efficiency practices. Meanwhile, high parts and Labor costs on repeat trucks suggest aging assets nearing replacement thresholds, which could inform proactive fleet investment decisions and prevent costly unplanned downtime, ultimately protecting the strong revenue base already built.

coffee sales project

Coffee dashboard reveals ₦35,396 in Net Sales, ₦20,779 Profit (59% margin), and 7,542 units sold, with a strong 4.42/5 customer rating. South West region drives 36% of sales, led by Lagos Island Cafe — showing heavy geographic concentration. Coffee itself accounts for 66% of revenue, with Latte and Cappuccino as top sellers and cost drivers. Medium-sized drinks are most profitable, and Walk-in customers dominate (56% of sales) over Students, Office Workers, and Remote Workers. Sunny days bring the most sales, while March and October peak seasonally. Diversifying regions, upselling to Large, and boosting loyalty programs could reduce reliance on one store/segment.

About

My training in International Relations means I approach data with a global, critical perspective — asking the right questions, communicating findings precisely, and focusing on insights that are genuinely useful to policymakers, organizations, and decision-makers alike.
